HSMSE Concentration Guide

A side-by-side analysis of all 5 tracks to help Andrew choose the right path for junior & senior year

High School for Math, Science & Engineering at City College — Spring 2026 Decision

Mathematics

For the student who sees beauty in theorems and proofs
  • College-credit courses from CCNY (PreCalc → Calculus)
  • AP Computer Science + Intro to Mathematical Reasoning
  • Senior: Linear Algebra, Advanced Topics (Graph/Number/Game Theory)
  • Required: 2 semesters Varsity Math Team

Engineering

For the builder, tinkerer, and hands-on problem solver
  • Project Lead the Way nationally validated curriculum
  • Digital Electronics + Principles of Engineering (Robotics)
  • Senior choices: Innovation & Design, Adv. Architecture, Networking
  • Paid summer internship at CCNY Grove School of Engineering

Science — Biology Focus

For the future biologist, doctor, or life-science researcher
  • AP Biology + Science Research course (junior year)
  • Senior: Genetics & Human Anatomy + Research Seminar
  • Guided independent research study with a science mentor
  • Present research findings at end of senior year

Science — Chemistry Focus

For the student drawn to molecular puzzles and lab work
  • AP Chemistry + Topics in Chemistry + Science Research (junior)
  • Senior: Organic Chemistry + Research Seminar
  • Guided independent research study with a science mentor
  • Strong prep for college-level chemistry & pre-med

Mt. Sinai Biomedical Research

For the aspiring physician-scientist — real hospital immersion
  • Half-day at Mt. Sinai Hospital campus (junior year)
  • Biomedical Sciences, Genetics & Molecular Biology
  • Senior: Clinical or research internship with a mentor doctor
  • Exposure to cardiology, oncology, obstetrics, autopsy suite

Mathematics Concentration

College-level abstract math + computer science + math team competition

Junior Year — Concentration Courses

  • Introduction to Math (college-credit from CCNY)
  • AP Computer Science
  • PreCalculus (A Day) / Gym-Lab (B Day)
  • US History / Physics
  • English
  • German or Spanish
  • Electives (both days)

Senior Year — Concentration Courses

  • Linear Algebra
  • Advanced Topics in Mathematics (Graph Theory, Number Theory, Game Theory, Maple)
  • AP Calculus (A Day) / Gym (B Day)
  • Government/Economics / Advanced Science
  • College Writing / Senior Research Paper
  • AP German or Elective
  • Senior Elective / OFF period

Pros

  • College credit from CCNY — head start on college transcript
  • Deepest math preparation of any track — Linear Algebra, Diff Eq, Advanced Topics
  • AP Computer Science gives valuable coding skills alongside pure math
  • Varsity Math Team builds competition experience (looks great on college apps)
  • Strongest foundation for STEM majors in college (CS, physics, engineering, economics, data science)
  • Broadest flexibility: math skills transfer to virtually any STEM or quantitative career
  • Develops abstract reasoning and proof-writing — key for top-tier university math/CS programs

Cons

  • No hands-on lab or real-world project component — purely theoretical
  • No internship or industry exposure built into the program
  • Requires genuine passion for abstract math — can feel disconnected if interests are more applied
  • Math Team requirement adds time commitment on top of coursework
  • Less differentiated on college apps vs. unique tracks like Mt. Sinai
  • No direct exposure to engineering design, biology, or medical fields

Best Fit For Andrew If…

He loves puzzles, proofs, and the elegance of mathematical reasoning. He's curious about computer science. He wants maximum flexibility for college — this track leaves every STEM door open (CS, engineering, physics, economics, data science, pure math). It's the "keep all options open" power move if he genuinely enjoys math.

Engineering Concentration

Project Lead the Way curriculum — nationally validated, hands-on engineering

Junior Year — Concentration Courses

  • Digital Electronics / Python Programming
  • Principles of Engineering — Robotics / Mechanical Design
  • PreCalculus/Calculus I (A Day) / Gym-Lab (B Day)
  • US History / Physics
  • AP English
  • German or Spanish
  • Electives (both days)

Senior Year — Concentration Courses

  • Senior Engineering — choose from:
  • Digital Systems Design (DSD)
  • Innovation & Design
  • Advanced Architecture
  • Networking & Environmental Sustainability
  • AP Calculus / Gym
  • Government/Economics / Advanced Science
  • College Writing / Senior Research Paper
  • Senior Elective / OFF period

Pros

  • Most hands-on track — robotics, mechanical design, electronics, prototyping
  • Paid summer internship opportunity at CCNY's Grove School of Engineering
  • Project Lead the Way is nationally recognized — colleges know and value it
  • Heavy teamwork and collaborative problem-solving (mirrors real engineering)
  • Multiple senior year specialization choices (architecture, innovation, networking)
  • Python programming integrated into Digital Electronics
  • Builds a portfolio of tangible projects for college applications
  • Direct pipeline to engineering programs at CCNY and beyond

Cons

  • Less advanced math compared to the Math track (no Linear Algebra / Diff Eq)
  • No AP science course specific to the concentration
  • More narrowly focused — less ideal if Andrew later pivots to medicine or pure science
  • Heavy project workload can conflict with other AP courses and extracurriculars
  • Does not include a formal research component
  • No biology or chemistry depth — harder to pivot to pre-med later

Best Fit For Andrew If…

He loves building things, enjoys tinkering with electronics or code, and wants to see ideas come to life as physical prototypes. The paid internship at CCNY is a genuine differentiator. Best if he's leaning toward mechanical, electrical, civil, or computer engineering in college.

Science Concentration — Biology Focus

AP Biology → Genetics & Human Anatomy → independent research with a mentor

Junior Year — Concentration Courses

  • AP Biology
  • Science Research
  • AP English / Gym-Lab
  • US History / Physics
  • Electives (both days)
  • German or Spanish / US History

Senior Year — Concentration Courses

  • Genetics / Human Anatomy
  • Science Research / Seminar (continues)
  • College Writing / Senior Research Paper / Gym
  • Government/Economics / Advanced Science
  • AP Calculus
  • AP German or Elective
  • Senior Elective / OFF period

Pros

  • Two full years of guided scientific research — rare at the high school level
  • AP Biology is a flagship AP course — strong college app signal
  • Genetics + Human Anatomy is pre-med gold (not offered at most high schools)
  • Produces a polished research paper suitable for science competitions (Regeneron, NYCSEF)
  • Science mentor provides one-on-one guidance — like a mini college research experience
  • Excellent preparation for biology, neuroscience, pre-med, public health in college
  • Stays within HSMSE campus — simpler logistics than Mt. Sinai

Cons

  • No hospital or clinical exposure (unlike Mt. Sinai)
  • Research is school-based, not embedded in a professional lab
  • Less math depth than the Math track
  • No engineering or hands-on building component
  • If Andrew isn't sure about bio, two years of it is a big commitment
  • Narrower than Chemistry focus for certain fields (pharmaceuticals, materials science)

Best Fit For Andrew If…

He's fascinated by living systems — how the body works, genetics, disease. He enjoys lab work and wants to develop real research skills. Ideal if he's considering pre-med, biology, neuroscience, or biomedical science in college, but wants to stay on the HSMSE campus full-time.

Science Concentration — Chemistry Focus

AP Chemistry → Organic Chemistry → independent research with a mentor

Junior Year — Concentration Courses

  • AP Chemistry
  • Topics in Chemistry (both A & B days)
  • Science Research
  • AP English / Gym-Lab
  • US History / Physics
  • German or Spanish / US History

Senior Year — Concentration Courses

  • Organic Chemistry
  • Science Research / Seminar (continues)
  • College Writing / Senior Research Paper
  • Government/Economics / Advanced Science
  • AP Calculus
  • AP German or Elective
  • Senior Elective / OFF period

Pros

  • Organic Chemistry in high school is extraordinary — most students first encounter it in college
  • Topics in Chemistry provides extra depth beyond AP (double period both days in junior year)
  • Two full years of guided scientific research with mentor
  • AP Chemistry score is highly valued by college STEM programs
  • Research paper for science competitions (Regeneron, NYCSEF)
  • Excellent prep for pre-med (Organic Chem is the notorious college "weed-out" — getting it early is an advantage)
  • Opens doors to chemistry, biochemistry, pharmacology, chemical engineering, materials science

Cons

  • Heaviest science course load — Topics in Chemistry occupies both A&B day slots in junior year
  • No hospital or clinical exposure
  • Organic Chemistry is notoriously difficult — very challenging at the high school level
  • Less flexibility for electives due to packed schedule (especially junior year)
  • Research is school-based, not embedded in a professional lab
  • If Andrew doesn't end up in a chemistry-adjacent field, the depth may feel excessive

Best Fit For Andrew If…

He lights up in chemistry class — loves reactions, molecular structures, and lab experiments. Organic Chemistry in high school is a serious differentiator for college apps and gives a massive head start for pre-med or any chemistry-intensive major. This is the most academically demanding science track.

Mt. Sinai Biomedical Research Program

Half-day hospital immersion → senior year clinical/research internship with a physician-mentor

Junior Year — Concentration Courses

  • Sinai Biomedical Sciences, Genetics & Molecular Biology (half-day at Mt. Sinai campus)
  • English / PreCalculus
  • Physics Elective (both A & B days)
  • German or Spanish / US History
  • Note: Mornings at Mt. Sinai, afternoons at HSMSE

Senior Year — Concentration Courses

  • Sinai Research or Clinical Rounds (with mentor doctor)
  • AP Calculus / College Writing / Senior Research Paper
  • Government/Economics / Advanced Science
  • AP German or Elective
  • Note: Research/clinical work spans periods 5-6

Pros

  • Most prestigious and unique track — real hospital immersion is extraordinary for a high schooler
  • Clinical rotations in cardiology, oncology, obstetrics, even autopsy suite
  • Paired one-on-one with a healthcare professional or researcher as a senior
  • Strongest possible signal on college apps for pre-med / biomedical science
  • Hands-on labs, lectures from actual physicians and researchers
  • Early career clarity — confirms (or rules out) a medical career before college commitment
  • Generates a competitive science research paper
  • The Mt. Sinai / Icahn School of Medicine name carries significant weight

Cons

  • Logistically complex — split schedule between HSMSE campus and Mt. Sinai hospital
  • Competitive admission within HSMSE — may not be guaranteed a spot
  • Most narrowly focused track — clearly oriented toward medicine/biomedical research
  • Less advanced math (PreCalculus in junior year, not college-level math like Math track)
  • If Andrew decides medicine isn't for him, other tracks offer broader preparation
  • Hospital environment can be emotionally intense (autopsy suite, oncology)
  • Less flexibility in schedule due to the hospital commute and fixed program blocks
  • No AP Biology or AP Chemistry within the concentration itself

Best Fit For Andrew If…

He's seriously drawn to medicine, biomedical research, or healthcare. This is the most immersive, "real-world" track — nothing else at HSMSE (or most high schools anywhere) gives a student this level of clinical exposure. The trade-off is that it's the most committed path: if medicine turns out not to be the right fit, pivoting is harder. But if it is the right fit, this is an unmatched opportunity.
